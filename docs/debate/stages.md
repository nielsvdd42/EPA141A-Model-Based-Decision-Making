# Debate Stages

The COP31 debate runs across **eight stages** spanning both sessions.

---

## Stage 1 — Introduction (Lecture 1, 20 April)

The debate is introduced during the first lecture. The JUSTICE model and available actors are presented.

---

## Stage 2 — Strategy building (homework)

Read your mandate. Run the JUSTICE analyses relevant to your actor's position. Identify your natural allies and your likely opponents. Begin reaching out to other groups via whatever communication channels you choose.

---

## Stage 3 — Bloc negotiations (Session 1, 13:15–14:00)

Six parallel bloc meetings take place simultaneously. Each bloc is responsible for keeping an **official record** of the meeting (notes). These notes are distributed to all bloc members after the meeting and may be shared with other actors at your discretion — they can be cited in the final debate.

**Bloc hosts responsible for note-keeping:**

| Bloc | Host |
|------|------|
| 1 — Existential Frontline | AOSIS representative |
| 2 — African & LDC | AGN representative |
| 3 — BASIC & Major Emerging Economies | China representative |
| 4 — High Ambition Coalition | EU representative |
| 5 — Fossil Fuel & Reluctant Emitters | USA representative |
| 6 — Transition Economies, Hosts & Brokers | Turkey representative (COP31 Host) |

---

## Stage 4 — Cross-bloc negotiation (Session 1, 14:00–14:45)

Open negotiation between blocs. No formal structure. Coordinate, form coalitions, and stress-test your arguments against opponents.

---

## Stage 5 — Preliminary plenary (Session 1, 14:45–15:15)

Short plenary led by the UNFCCC Chair. The Chair presents the current draft agreement (or outlines priorities). This stage rehearses the format of the final debate and allows each actor to voice concerns.

---

## Stage 6 — Policy building (homework, between sessions)

Continue negotiation and analysis. The UNFCCC Chair prepares a policy proposal for Session 2. Each group must submit a **one-page pre-debate reflection** by **email to [J.ZatarainSalazar@tudelft.nl](mailto:J.ZatarainSalazar@tudelft.nl)** before **17:00 on 9 June**, with subject line:

> `[EPA141A <Group Number> Pre-Debate Reflection]`

The reflection should address:
- Your actual goals for the final agreement, as specific as possible — cite your JUSTICE analysis
- Who you believe are your natural allies and why
- Who you expect will oppose you and on what grounds
- Your strategy to achieve your goals in the final debate, including how you plan to use the modelling results

```{note}
The pre-debate reflection is **not graded** but is **compulsory**.
```

---

## Stage 7 — Final plenary debate (Session 2, 13:00–15:15)

Each bloc is represented by **one spokesperson** at the table. That spokesperson may ask other members of their group to be given the floor; the Chair grants permission. The rest of the class observes and supports their spokesperson via any communication channel, runs additional analysis, drafts arguments, and relays information. Actors may present visualisations or model outputs during the debate.

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| 13:00–13:15 | UNFCCC Chair presents final draft agreement with proposed emission targets and projected outcomes |
| 13:15–13:55 | First round of debate |
| 13:55–14:15 | Break — spokespersons consult groups, negotiate bilaterally |
| 14:15–14:55 | Second round of debate |
| 14:55–15:15 | Chair updates agreement if warranted; **clusters vote** (majority of 4 required to adopt) |

```{note}
All six clusters cast **one vote each**. A majority of **four votes** is required to adopt the agreement.
The UNFCCC Chair (Cluster 6) moderates and does not vote, but may reject the agreement if it is
incompatible with the UNFCCC mandate.
```

---

## Stage 8 — Debrief (15:15–15:25)

We debrief on the entire process, highlight good strategies observed throughout the debate, and analyse strategies that succeeded in de-valuing models and analysis in favour of political arguments — and how those could have been overcome.
